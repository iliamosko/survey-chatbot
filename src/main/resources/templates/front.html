Consent Form The purpose of this research study is to understand how people interact electronically using computer mediated communication. You will complete a collaborative task with the help of a virtual partner. For the duration of the task, you will have Skype chat open to communicate with your partner. You will then be asked to respond to some questions regarding your experience and views about your partner. At the end of the questionnaire, you will also be asked to provide information about yourself and your beliefs, in general.

This research study will take about 30 minutes to complete, and you will not be compensated for your participation.
Your participation is voluntary and greatly appreciated. You may choose not to participate at all or you may refuse to participate in certain procedures, answer certain questions, or discontinue your participation at any time without consequences. Refusal to answer any particular questions or withdrawal from the study will not affect you in any way. You have the right to withdraw without any penalty. Also, you have the right to request that your responses not be used in the data analyses.

Participation in this research study does not involve any foreseeable risks.

You will experience no direct benefits from this study. However, one potential benefit of participating in this research study is that you have the opportunity to learn more about the research process and also help contribute to scientific advancement.

This experiment is anonymous and no personally identifiable information will be stored with your responses. Your confidentiality will be protected to the maximum extent allowed by law. All data will be stored on the hard drive of a secure computer, and on a secure server. Data will only be accessed by trained experimenters. Data will be stored for up to five years after the publication of research stemming from this project---as specified by the American Psychological Association.

At the conclusion of this research the experimenter will provide a thorough explanation of the study. It is our goal that you learn about the research you participated in today. Furthermore, the experimenter will be happy to answer any questions you have about the research.

Brent Lyons, a professor in the Schulich School of Business at York University is conducting this scientific study. If you have questions about the study, contact Brent Lyons, Ph.D., Schulich School of Business, York University via e-mail: blyons@schulich.yorku.ca.
